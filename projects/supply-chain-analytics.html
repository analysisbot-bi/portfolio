Supply Chain Performance Analytics & Delay-Risk Prediction

A web decision-support system connected to Power BI. It tracks shipment performance and scores delay risk so teams can act before a late delivery shows up in a monthly report.

XGBoostSHAPPower BI Predictive AnalyticsPythonStreamlit

The problem

Delays often show up too late. By the time they hit a monthly report, you already have missed deliveries, stuck inventory, and unhappy customers. I wanted a tool that gives live visibility and an early warning on delay risk, without needing a data science background to use it.

My approach

I built a pipeline from raw trade/customs shipment data to a risk score and KPI layer:

  • Preprocessing: log-transforms on skewed value/mass features so outliers don't dominate the model.
  • Feature engineering: HS commodity chapter, origin/destination encodings, trade-route features, and usage flags.
  • Tuned XGBoost classifier for eligibility / delay-risk prediction, tested on ~44k held-out shipments.
  • SHAP: beeswarm and waterfall plots so predictions can be explained.
  • KPI dashboard: eligibility mix, high-risk origins/commodities, and ML risk-score distribution.

Results

Tuned XGBoost hit 86.9% accuracy and ROC-AUC 0.96 on the test set. SHAP showed usage flags, country of origin, trade route, and HS commodity chapter as the top drivers. About 36% of shipments landed in the not-eligible band, mostly in a few commodity chapters and origins.

Takeaways

The useful part was not just the model score. It was getting a risk signal into a dashboard form that ops people can read. Good analytics is the model plus the delivery format.
