Obesity Level Classification Using Artificial Neural Networks

Predict obesity category from lifestyle / body-metric data. I trained three ANN setups and compared accuracy vs how well they generalised.

ANNDeep LearningMulti-class Classification DropoutL2 RegularisationPython

The problem

Category depends on several things (diet, activity, history, habits), not one number. Goal: put someone into one of seven classes.

What I did

Standard supervised classification with neural nets. Prep first:

  • Encode categorical lifestyle fields.
  • Scale features with StandardScaler.
  • Train/test split so scores are on unseen rows.

Then three networks:

  • Baseline: small net.
  • Deep: more layers.
  • Regularised: deep + dropout + L2.

Results

Regularised deep model won at about 95% test accuracy (baseline was ~87%). Train/val curves stayed close. Confusion matrix + learning curves were more useful than staring at one accuracy figure.

Notes

Extra layers alone weren't enough. Dropout and L2 helped more. And yeah, always look at the confusion matrix.

Headline results

95% test accuracy (regularised Deep ANN)
7 obesity categories classified
3 architectures compared
